Medical certificate guide for student pilots

Medical & CertificationAugust 11, 20264 min read

Quick answer

Private Pilot students need at least a third-class medical certificate before solo. Sport Pilot students may use a driver’s license instead. Discovery flights require no medical. The exam is straightforward for most healthy adults and is conducted by an FAA Aviation Medical Examiner.

The medical certificate is one of the first administrative steps new student pilots worry about. The good news is that the requirements are simpler than most people expect, and you do not need perfect health or perfect vision. This guide explains what you need, when you need it, and how to get it in the Reno area.

When you need a medical certificate

You do not need a medical to start training. You can take a discovery flight, start lessons, and log dual instruction without one. You only need the appropriate medical before you fly solo, which typically happens after 15–25 hours of instruction.

For the Private Pilot certificate, you need at least a third-class medical. For the Sport Pilot certificate, you can usually fly using your driver’s license in place of an FAA medical, provided you have not been denied or had a medical revoked.

What the exam covers

An Aviation Medical Examiner, or AME, is a physician the FAA has authorized to issue medical certificates. The exam includes:

  • A vision test
  • A color vision check
  • A hearing test
  • Blood pressure measurement
  • A review of your medical history and current medications
  • A basic physical exam, including heart rhythm

Most healthy adults pass without issue. Conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, or certain mental health diagnoses are not automatic disqualifiers, but they may require documentation. If you have a concern, talk to the AME before the exam.

Third-class medical duration

A third-class medical is valid for five years if you are under 40, and two years if you are 40 or older. The certificate itself also acts as your student pilot certificate during training. After you earn your Private Pilot certificate, the same medical lets you exercise those privileges.

BasicMed explained

After you have held at least a third-class medical at any point, you may be eligible for BasicMed instead of renewing a third-class medical. BasicMed requires:

  • A physical exam by any state-licensed physician every four years
  • An online medical education course every two years
  • Operating within BasicMed limits, such as flying aircraft under 6,000 pounds with no more than five passengers

BasicMed is popular among Private Pilots who fly for personal use and do not need commercial privileges. It does not replace the third-class medical for initial Private Pilot training.

How to find an AME in Reno

You can search for local AMEs on the FAA’s MedXPress website. We can also point students toward trusted examiners in the Reno area. Contact us at 1008 Gentry Way, Reno, NV 89512, or call +1-555-555-1234, and we will share current recommendations.

What if I wear glasses or contact lenses?

Corrective lenses are fine. You simply need to demonstrate that your vision is correctable to the required standard while wearing them. Most pilots who wear glasses pass the vision test without issue.

When to schedule your medical

Do not wait until the day before your first solo. Schedule the exam a few weeks ahead so you have time to provide any additional paperwork if the AME requests it. If you are considering a career in aviation, you may want to get a first-class medical early to confirm you can meet the higher standards.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting too long. Medical issues are easier to resolve when you have time.
  • Hiding conditions. Be honest with the AME. The FAA values transparency, and many conditions can be managed with the right documentation.
  • Assuming one failure ends your dream. Many pilots obtain a medical after initially failing by providing additional information or obtaining a special issuance.

Bottom line

For most students, the medical certificate is a brief appointment, not a barrier. Get it before solo, keep track of the expiration date, and ask your CFI if you are unsure which path fits your goals. Read our complete flight training in Reno guide for the full training picture.
