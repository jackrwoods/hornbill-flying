FAA medical certificate guide

Medical certification is a common early hurdle. Here is what you need, where to schedule an exam, and how to handle typical questions.

Which medical certificate do I need?

CertificateTypical useTraining path
Third classPrivate Pilot, Sport Pilot, recreational flyingPPL, SPL, most initial training
Second classCommercial pilot operationsCommercial Pilot
First classAirline transport pilotATP and some career-track planning
BasicMedLimited Private Pilot privileges after a medical courseNot for student pilot solo or IFR training

How to schedule your exam

  1. Complete your application in FAA MedXPress before your appointment.
  2. Find an Aviation Medical Examiner with the FAA AME locator.
  3. Bring your ID, MedXPress confirmation number, and a list of any medications.

Common medical questions

Can I wear glasses?
Yes. Corrective lenses are allowed for all classes of medical certificates. Your certificate will simply note that you must wear them while flying.
What about ADHD or common medications?
Some diagnoses and medications require extra documentation or a special issuance. Talk to an AME early so you know what to bring.
Is BasicMed enough for flight training?
BasicMed does not authorize student pilot solos or Instrument Rating training. Most students start with a third-class medical.

Student resources FAQ

Quick answers to the questions we hear most from current and enrolled students.

How do I check the weather before a flight at RNO?

Start with the live KRNO METAR and TAF widget on this page, then open a full briefing on the FAA Aviation Weather Center or call 1-800-WX-BRIEF. Always obtain an official preflight briefing before every flight.

Where can I find the current PA28 checklist?

Download the shared PA28 checklist from the Training syllabi and aircraft documents section below. Carry the physical checklist in the aircraft and confirm it matches the current POH.

What FAA medical certificate do I need for a Private Pilot?

A third-class medical certificate is required for a Private Pilot certificate. You can also operate under BasicMed for many Private Pilot privileges after completing the required medical course.

Can I use BasicMed for flight training?

BasicMed works for many training flights, but it does not cover all privileges. A traditional third-class medical is usually simpler for students working toward a Private Pilot certificate or Instrument Rating.

Where are the local practice areas around Reno?

The common practice area lies northeast of RNO over the desert, outside the Class C shelf. See the practice area map tool for a visual reference, and always verify current sectional charts and NOTAMs before departing.

How do I estimate cross-country fuel for the PA28?

Use the cross-country estimator tool to enter origin, destination, cruise speed, and fuel burn. It adds a 30-minute day VFR reserve. Cross-check the result with your POH and current winds before filing.

Who do I contact if a syllabus link is broken?

Call or text Hornbill Aviation at 555-555-1234, or email office@hornbillaviation.com. We will send you the correct document and fix the link.
